How much does the translation behavior of a language indicate about its culture? Do German speakers seek the same words translated as the Spanish? To investigate, all the single word translations of nouns1 & adjectives2 into English requested through Google Translate were analyzed, looking at 10 of the most popular languages found on Google. In this setting, the most common word translated from Russian into English is mama, while many French speakers want to know the translation of bien into English, meaning good.

1 e.g. house, dog, place
2 e.g. easy, intelligent, adorable

Translations for you, hello and thank you into English are three of the most requested words in any language, which isn't unexpected. By focusing on the nouns & adjectives instead, we can get a glimpse into how different non-native English speakers use this language in their everyday lives.

Diving even deeper and looking at the 10 most translated words※, some interesting differences arise between the chosen languages. The days of the week are quite popular, especially in Spanish & Italian. For the French, monsieur is on 6th place, which Google translates to gentleman, seemingly quite fitting for their culture.
