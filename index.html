How much does the translation behavior of a language indicate about its culture? Do German speakers seek the same words translated as the Spanish? To investigate, we've taken all the single word translations of nouns1 & adjectives2 into English, requested through Google translate, from 10 of the most popular languages found on Google. For example, the most common word translated from Russian into English is mama, while many French speakers want to know the translation of bien into English, meaning good.

1 e.g. house, dog, place
2 e.g. easy, intelligent, adorable; saying something about a noun

Google Translate is used my millions of people around the World. If we take out all the hello's, thank you's, I, we, she's, no's, but's and similar grammatical types of words we are left with an interesting collection of nouns and their adjectives that people want translated.

Diving even deeper and looking at the top 10 most translated words some interesting differences arise between the chosen languages. The days of the week are quite popular, especially in Spanish & Italian. For the French, monsieur is on 6th place, which Google translates to gentleman, seemingly quite fitting for their culture. Luckily, the most often translated word across all languages is the wonderful word beautiful, or, as they say in German, schon
